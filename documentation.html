Technical Indicators

Technical indicator is a mathematical calculation which is based on the historic price, volume, and/or open interest of a stock/security and used by technical analysts/traders for technical analysis to predict probable future price movements. In technical analysis a stock's trading history is recorded by maintaining volumes traded and price changes in a chart and these technical indicators are additions to a chart that helps to predict the market trend.

  • Technical indicators are the interpreters of the stock market as they look at price information and translate it into easy to read signals that can help traders to determine when to buy and when to sell the stocks.
  • It helps the trader to identify the direction of future prices and alert the trader about the market trend.
  • Technical indicators are widely used by traders in the market as they are primarily designed for analysing short-term price movements.Thus, they are not quite useful for fundamental analysis like business earnings, revenue and profit margins.
  • Though, each technical indicator provides unique information but they look at historical price data. Therefore, they are not guaranteed to know anything definite about the future.


Categories of Technical Indicators

Leading

Lagging


  • Leading Indicator - A Leading indicator is a tool to predict the future direction of market so that traders can predict market movements ahead of time. Theoretically, it gives the correct signal that a trader can get before the market movement. However, leading indicators are not 100% accurate, that is why they are always used in combination with other forms of technical analysis.

  • Lagging Indicator - A lagging indicator is a tool that gives a signal after the trend i.e., the price movement has already passed or reversal has started. Before entering into a trade, traders used lagging indicators to confirm the price trend.

Types of Technical Indicators

Indicators from LEADING and LAGGING categories belong to one of the following types:

  1. Trend Indicators
  2. Momentum Indicators
  3. Volatility Indicators
  4. Volume Indicators

Trend Indicators

Trend indicators uses some form of price averaging to form a baseline and predicts the direction and strength of a trend. When the price moves above the average, it can be considered as a bullish trend. If the price moves below the average then it signals a bearish trend. These trend indicators are helpful as they help traders to trade in the same direction as the underlying trend and moreover,they also signals a change in trend which enables trader to enter a position right at the start of that new trend.
Most widely used trend indicators are Moving Averages(MA) and Moving Average Convergence Divergence(MACD) which are lagging trend indicators and Parabolic Stop and Reverse(Parabolic SAR) is a leading trend indicator.


Momentum Indicators

Momentum indicators shows the movement of price over time and strength of the movement regardless of the direction of the price movement. It is used by traders to identify the speed or rate at which the price of a stock changes. It is calculated by comparing the current closing price to previous closing prices. Typically, it appears as a line below price chart that oscillates as momentum changes. It can signal change in future prices, if there is a divergence between price movement and momentum.
Most commonly used momentum indicators are Stochastic Oscillator, Commodity Channel Index(CCI) and Relative Strength Index(RSI) and they all are leading momentum indicators.


Volatility Indicators

Volatility indicators identifies the rate of price movement regardless of direction. It is based on the change in the highest and lowest historical prices. Volatility indicator helps the trader to measure the state of a market and to identify possible breakouts from a range bound market.
Few examples of volatility indicators are Bollinger Bands, Average True Range and Standard Deviation and they all are lagging volatility indicators.


Volume Indicators

Volume indicators helps to identify the strength of a trend or confirm the trading direction based on some form of averaging or smoothing of raw volume. The strongest trends formed when volume increases and the trading volume increment is the key factor which can lead to large movements in price.
Examples of volume indicators are Chaikin Oscillator, On-Balance Volume(OBV) which are leading Volume indicators and Volume Rate of Change is a lagging volume indicator.
